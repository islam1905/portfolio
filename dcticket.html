DC Ticket App

Design an app that allow people to find, contest, and pay their parking tickets.

Project Goals

The lack of an app or system for people to view, pay, and contest their parking tickets.

Target Audience

Creating a more accessible ticket application for people. This way, they can easily access the app, view and review their tickets, contest them, and make payments when necessary.

User Research

Conducted extensive research including surveys (150 participants) and interviews (20 users) to understand pain points in ticket purchasing. Key findings showed that 68% of users found existing apps confusing, and 82% wanted a simpler checkout process. Competitive analysis revealed opportunities to differentiate our solution.

Persona

Developed three primary personas based on research data. The main persona, "Tech-Savvy Tom," represents our target demographic - 25-35 year old professionals who frequently attend events but are frustrated with current booking experiences. Personas guided all design decisions to ensure user needs were prioritized.

User Scenarios & Journeys

Created detailed user flows mapping the complete ticket purchase process from discovery to post-event. Identified critical pain points where users dropped off (particularly during seat selection and payment). Designed solutions to streamline these journeys, reducing steps from 7 to 4 in the primary flow.

Information Architecture

Developed a hierarchical structure prioritizing event discovery and quick booking. Card sorting with 15 participants helped optimize categories. The final IA reduced menu items from 12 to 7 while improving findability (success rate increased from 65% to 92% in testing).

Wireframes

Style Guide & UI Kit

Developed a comprehensive design system including color palette (primary brand color #FF6B6B), typography (Inter font family), spacing system (8px grid), and component library. Ensured AA accessibility compliance for all interactive elements. The UI kit accelerated development and maintained consistency.

Prototyping

Built clickable prototypes to test core flows before visual design. Conducted 12 usability tests identifying 3 major navigation issues. Iterated to improve success rates from 70% to 95% on primary tasks. This phase saved approximately 40 hours of development time by catching issues early.

Usability Testing

Conducted two rounds of moderated testing with 8 participants each. Measured task completion rates, time-on-task, and satisfaction scores. Addressed all critical issues (like confusing seat selection) before moving to high-fidelity design. SUS score improved from 68 to 89 through iterations.

Refining the Design

Implemented changes based on testing feedback: simplified navigation, added progress indicators, and redesigned the checkout flow. Conducted A/B testing on key screens to validate improvements. The refined design reduced user errors by 60% and improved completion rates by 45%.

Mockups

Created pixel-perfect mockups for all key screens incorporating the visual design system. Included multiple states (default, hover, active, disabled) for interactive elements. Developed responsive layouts for mobile, tablet, and desktop to ensure consistent experience across devices.

High-fidelity Prototype

Built an interactive prototype simulating the complete user experience with realistic animations and transitions. Used for final stakeholder reviews and developer handoff. The prototype achieved 98% task completion in validation testing, confirming readiness for development.
